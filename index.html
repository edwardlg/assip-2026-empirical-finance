    Welcome¶

    I am Lei Gao, Associate Professor of Finance at George Mason University's Costello College of Business, and I will be your mentor for the next eight weeks. The work you are about to do is not a summer-camp simulation of research — it is research. By August 12 each of you will have written a pre-analysis plan, executed a real specification on real data, run the robustness tests that separate findings from artifacts, and defended your numbers in front of GMU faculty, your sub-team peers, and (often) the families who drove you to symposium day.

    We will be remote — every meeting on Zoom, every commit on GitHub, every draft in the shared repo — but the standard is the same one I hold my PhD students to. The textbook, the data, and the office hours are all set up so a motivated high-school senior who has taken AP Statistics can do this. The only thing you cannot outsource is showing up, every weekday, with your laptop and last night's notebook ready to discuss. Do that, and you will surprise yourself by August.

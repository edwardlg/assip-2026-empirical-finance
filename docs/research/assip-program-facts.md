# ASSIP 2026 Program Facts

Research notes compiled for Prof. Lei Gao (Associate Professor of Finance, Costello
College of Business, GMU) preparing to mentor 14 high-school interns in empirical
finance research during Summer 2026.

Compiled: June 2026. All claims sourced from official GMU College of Science pages
unless otherwise noted.

## 3. Audience and Eligibility

- **High school students AND undergraduates** with STEM interests. No grade-level
  floor is published; the binding criterion is age.
- **Age minimums** (must be reached by June 18, 2026):
  - 15+ for *remote* internships and in-person computer/data labs
  - 16+ for *wet-lab* in-person or hybrid internships (biological/chemical hazard
    insurance requirement)
- **No maximum age**, provided the applicant has not yet graduated from a
  four-year university.
- **Maximum lifetime participation:** two summer sessions.
- **No minimum GPA**; transcripts and letters of recommendation are not requested
  or accepted at the initial review stage.
- For Prof. Gao's empirical-finance cohort (remote, no hazardous materials), the
  effective audience is **rising 10th–12th graders aged 15+, plus undergraduates**.

Source: <https://science.gmu.edu/assip/how-apply>, <https://science.gmu.edu/assip/faqs>

## 4. Daily / Weekly Structure

- **Full-time, in-person business-hours commitment:** Monday–Friday, **9:00 am –
  5:00 pm** (effectively 30+ hours/week; the FAQ phrases it as "work normal
  business hours of 9:00 to 5:00 pm").
- Continuous participation is required; students may not miss extended periods.
- **Format negotiated mentor-by-mentor** — each mentor declares in advance
  whether the slot is *in-person only*, *hybrid*, or *fully remote*.
- **Locations** (in-person mentors):
  - **Fairfax (Main) Campus** — primary site
  - **SciTech Campus (Manassas)**
  - **Potomac Science Center (Woodbridge)**
  - **Mason Square (Arlington)** — newly added for 2026 cycle
- Free Mason shuttles between campuses; students are responsible for their own
  travel. Limited on-campus housing for adults (18+) at student expense; no
  housing for minors.

Note for Prof. Gao: his 2026 mentor listing is **Remote only**, so his 14 students
will participate virtually from their homes (subject to a synchronous 9-5 EDT
expectation).

Source: <https://science.gmu.edu/assip/faqs>, <https://science.gmu.edu/assip/prospective-2026-assip-mentors>

## 5. Mentor Model and Cohort Size

- **Total cohort size:** ASSIP has grown into one of the nation's largest summer
  research internships. The 2023 cohort exceeded **~300 interns** working with
  **100+ Mason STEM faculty/staff mentors**. The 2026 cohort is expected to be
  of similar scale.
- **Mentor-to-mentee structure:** Officially described as students working
  "one-on-one with faculty researchers." In practice, individual mentors take on
  **varying group sizes** (often **2–15 interns per mentor**, depending on the
  research project's capacity). The official site does not publish a fixed
  ratio — each mentor sets capacity in their 2026 listing.
- Prof. Gao is hosting **14 mentees** in 2026 — a large group by ASSIP norms but
  feasible because (a) the work is remote and (b) empirical finance projects
  scale via parallel student-level work on shared CRSP/Compustat/SEC EDGAR data.
- Mentors span 25+ academic areas; the 2026 list includes three Costello /
  Schar business-school mentors: **Jiasun Li** (blockchain/FinTech), **Derek
  Horstmeyer** (ETF performance), and **Lei Gao** (Economics of AI, Market
  Structure, Investments, Corporate Finance, FinTech — *Remote only*).

Sources: <https://science.gmu.edu/news/high-schoolers-gain-hands-research-experience-and-college-credit-george-mason-aspiring-0>, <https://science.gmu.edu/assip/prospective-2026-assip-mentors>

## 6. Required Student Deliverables

1. **Research poster** — the headline deliverable. Every intern presents a
   scientific poster at the August 12 Research Symposium.
2. **Research abstract** — abstracts from accepted projects (2019 onward) are
   published in the **Journal of Student-Scientists' Research (JSSR)**, an
   open-access GMU Libraries Mason Publishing journal.
3. **Optional full paper** — projects meeting publication standards may submit a
   complete research article to JSSR. Some interns also co-author conference
   papers or peer-reviewed journal articles with their mentors.
4. **Career-development participation** — career exploration forums and
   scientific-writing workshops are embedded in the 8 weeks.
5. **3 college credits from George Mason University** are earned for successful
   completion (counted as a Mason undergraduate research course on transcript).
6. **Cost:** $25 nonrefundable application fee + $1,299 tuition. Fee waivers
   (full coverage of both) available for Pell-eligible / reduced-lunch students.

Sources: <https://science.gmu.edu/assip>, <https://journals.gmu.edu/jssr/issue/archive>

## 7. End-of-Summer Symposium

- **Name:** ASSIP Research Symposium (also styled "ASSIP Poster Session").
- **Date:** Wednesday, **August 12, 2026** (the final program day).
- **Format:** In-person poster session at GMU; remote interns are also expected
  to present (typically via uploaded poster + virtual session for fully remote
  mentor groups; the program has supported hybrid presentation modes since
  pandemic-era expansion).
- **Audience:** Mason faculty, mentors, family/friends of interns, College of
  Science staff, university leadership, and the broader Mason research
  community. Press coverage is common (the Mason News office routinely
  features standout posters).
- **Companion deliverable:** Polished abstracts go to JSSR for archival
  publication.

Sources: <https://science.gmu.edu/assip>, <https://science.gmu.edu/assip/how-apply>

## 8. Key Differences vs. GMU Schar NextGen FinTech Scholars

Both programs run at George Mason in summer 2026 and both target high-school
students interested in finance/data work, but they are structurally very
different:

| Dimension | **ASSIP** (College of Science) | **NextGen FinTech Scholars** (Schar School) |
|---|---|---|
| **Hosting unit** | GMU College of Science | Schar School of Policy & Government, Young Scholars Research Program (executive education) |
| **Scope** | Broad STEM — all sciences, engineering, math, plus some business/finance mentors | Narrow vertical: FinTech / AI / data analytics in finance |
| **Length** | **~8 weeks** (June 22 – Aug 12, 2026) | **~12 weeks** (June 26 – Sept 11, 2026) |
| **Intensity** | **Full-time, Mon–Fri 9–5** | **Friday-only, 12:00–2:00 PM EST**, Phase 1 weeks of June 26 – Aug 7 |
| **Mode** | Lab-based / in-person at Fairfax, SciTech, Potomac, Arlington — with remote slots per mentor | Live online + a hybrid Phase-2 conference day (Saturday, Aug 15, 2026) |
| **Cohort** | ~300 interns, 100+ mentors | Cohort-based class, small teams; team competition at Phase-2 |
| **Mentor model** | One faculty mentor (with possibly multiple student mentees) | Instructor-led course with project teams |
| **Deliverables** | Research poster + JSSR abstract/paper + symposium | Research paper (due Sept 11) + team presentation at Aug-15 hybrid conference |
| **Credit / cost** | 3 GMU undergraduate credits; $25 app fee + $1,299 tuition (waivers available) | Non-credit certificate program; tuition fee + $500 scholarships for winning teams |
| **Application** | Highly competitive central application, opens Dec 8 / closes Feb 15 | Schar executive-education enrollment via Young Scholars Research Program |
| **Symposium** | ASSIP Research Symposium, Aug 12 (Wednesday) | Phase 2 hybrid conference, Saturday Aug 15 |

**Bottom line:** ASSIP is a *full-time, multi-week, research-apprenticeship*
experience in the style of an REU; NextGen FinTech Scholars is a *part-time,
weekly seminar/cohort course* with a culminating paper-and-team-competition.
Prof. Gao's 14-student empirical-finance group sits inside ASSIP — meaning
those students will commit Mon–Fri 9–5 for 8 weeks (remote), produce posters
+ JSSR abstracts, and earn 3 Mason credits.

Source for NextGen comparison: <https://schar.gmu.edu/programs/executive-education/young-scholars-research-program/nextgen-fintech-scholars-data-science>

## 9. Implications for Prof. Gao's 14-Student Empirical Finance Cohort

- The 14 interns will be a **mix of high-school sophomores–seniors (15+) and
  some undergrads**, working **remotely** to Prof. Gao's lab over 8 weeks.
- They are full-time (9–5 EDT) and must clear Mason's onboarding by June 18.
- **Each intern needs a poster** for August 12. With 14 students, project
  scaffolding should produce 14 distinct posters (likely organized as parallel
  variations on a shared empirical-finance theme — e.g., different industries,
  different sample windows, different identification strategies).
- **JSSR submission** is a realistic stretch goal — Prof. Gao's "8-Week Camp"
  textbook/labs in `/mnt/e/ccli/8weeks` are well-aligned with producing
  publishable abstracts.
- Because mentors are not given a fixed ratio, **14 mentees is supported but
  high** — Prof. Gao should plan for layered mentorship (himself + GMU PhD
  students / RAs / TAs as group leaders for sub-teams of 2–3 interns each).
- Symposium logistics: remote-cohort posters historically participate via
  uploaded PDF + a virtual breakout; confirm Aug-12 modality with the ASSIP
  office early in July.

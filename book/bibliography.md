## A — Empirical-finance classics (Weeks 5–6)

These five papers are the "anchor canon" the cohort reads cover-to-cover in
Weeks 5 and 6. Reader's Guides for each are in `book/weeks/week-05/` and
`book/weeks/week-06/`.

- **Fama, E. F. & French, K. R. (1992).** *The Cross-Section of Expected Stock
  Returns.* Journal of Finance **47**(2), 427–465.
  JSTOR: <https://www.jstor.org/stable/2329112>
- **Fama, E. F. & French, K. R. (1993).** *Common Risk Factors in the Returns
  on Stocks and Bonds.* Journal of Financial Economics **33**(1), 3–56.
  DOI: <https://doi.org/10.1016/0304-405X(93)90023-5>
- **Jegadeesh, N. & Titman, S. (1993).** *Returns to Buying Winners and
  Selling Losers: Implications for Stock Market Efficiency.*
  Journal of Finance **48**(1), 65–91.
  JSTOR: <https://www.jstor.org/stable/2328882>
- **Petersen, M. A. (2009).** *Estimating Standard Errors in Finance Panel
  Data Sets: Comparing Approaches.* Review of Financial Studies **22**(1),
  435–480. DOI: <https://doi.org/10.1093/rfs/hhn053>
- **Bertrand, M., Duflo, E., & Mullainathan, S. (2004).** *How Much Should We
  Trust Differences-in-Differences Estimates?* Quarterly Journal of Economics
  **119**(1), 249–275. DOI: <https://doi.org/10.1162/003355304772839588>
